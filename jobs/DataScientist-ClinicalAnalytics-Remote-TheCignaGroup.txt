Position title: Data Scientist - Clinical Analytics - Remote

Company: The Cigna Group


The Clinical Analytics Team within Evernorth’s Digital & Analytics (D&A) organization is expanding its deployment of analytics across the business as we fulfill our customer-centric mission to help the people we serve improve their health and well-being.  The clinical Analytics team provides the ability to visualize, articulate, and solve complex and complicated business problems and identify opportunities for innovation for US based business lines including Medical Management (Care+), Behavioral Management, Pharmacy, Operations, Clinical programs and Pricing/Affordability.  These businesses are aligned under our health improvement and retail strategies, all with the common goal of identifying what works best for our customers.Our team supports the business by applying the following techniques to answer these key business questions:Data mining and pattern recognition to answer “what happened?” and “where are the opportunities?”Root cause statistical analyses to answer “why did this happen?”Predictive modeling to answer “what will happen next?”, “who is it likely to happen to?”, and “when will it happen?”Experimental design to test new solutions in order to answer “how can we get better?”By partnering with the business lines, these analytic competencies enable efficient and effective decision making to allow us to constantly improve and create evidence-based customer-focus solutions. The department also has overall accountability for the methods and standards used to create actionable, proof-of-value statements for all the clients. As such, we set the standards for the methods that apply externally in reporting the value of Evernorth services to the client and ensure that results can be used to make material changes to the client’s covered population.ROLE SUMMARY:The Data Scientist position is an opportunity for analytics professional to provide leadership and initiatives on complex analytics projects. Reporting to the Business Analytics Sr. Manager, Clinical Analytics, this role will work collaboratively with our Evernorth Care Solutions business stakeholders to provide partnership in achieving Evernorth's growth goals through development of analytically driven product solutionsThe ideal candidate is someone with strong data science and programming background, previous experience in the healthcare industry, statistical acumen, and the ability to effectively communicate data insights and recommendations to business stakeholders. He/she is expected to have fundamental understanding of different statistical modeling techniques, economic evaluation concepts, and general health service research.  The selected candidate will interface with clinical, reporting, product development, operations, and marketing departments as appropriate.The JOB RESPONSIBILITIES include, but are not limited to the following:Collaborate with business partners within clinical, operations and marketing to determine the best approach for testing the effectiveness of new/existing programsGuide the development and analysis of a case management program, from proof of concept, data management to data analysis, interpretation, and presentation to internal and external stakeholdersExtraction and analysis of large healthcare claims data, conduct opportunity analysis including descriptive and multivariate statistics to identify patterns in the dataPresentation of results and recommendations to non-technical business partners and stakeholders to drive the decision-making process and support business operations for internal and external customers.The ideal candidate for this role will be highly motivated, flexible and embrace change.  In addition, the candidate will know evaluation research design techniques and their strengths and weaknesses. This role typically requires BS or MS in Biostatistics, Health Economics, Health Services Research, Epidemiology or equivalent training and mastery.IDEAL CANDIDATES WILL OFFER:Strong customer focus and management of client expectationsAdvanced degree or equivalent preferred1-3 years of experience in related analytics strongly preferredExperience with statistical software: SAS (SAS Enterprise Guide is a plus), R and/or PythonDeep healthcare data (e.g., ICD-9/ICD-10, claims, profiling), statistical analysis experience, and demonstrated understanding of health care and delivery system processesLeadership competencies with the ability to collaborate with others and establish working relationships, communicate effectively across the organization and at different levels, think and act strategically, and influence key leadersTranslate business needs into practical applications and solutionsWorking knowledge of program evaluation, predictive modeling, data mining, steerage, pharmacy and care managementAbility to successfully navigate and contribute in a highly-matrixed environmentEmerging track record of presentations / publicationsIf you will be working at home occasionally or permanently, the internet connection must be obtained through a cable broadband or fiber optic internet service provider with speeds of at least 10Mbps download/5Mbps upload.For this position, we anticipate offering an annual salary of 95,200 - 158,600 USD / yearly, depending on relevant factors, including experience and geographic location.This role is also anticipated to be eligible to participate in an annual bonus plan.We want you to be healthy, balanced, and feel secure.
